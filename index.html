Swift and my love for Apple

When I had to describe myself, a perfectionist or at least sophisticated in terms of details would come to mind. That's also how I would describe Apple. So it's quite obvious that I have a certain passion for Apple's products and technologies. When I took my first steps in the vast and sometimes overwhelming programming cosmos many years ago, it was a logical step for me to write programs for Apple products. However, at that time I had the the shocking realisation that Objective-C seemed rather old-fashioned and outdated. And, unlike the products at that time, I was not at all enthusiastic about it. This and the high entry barrier unfortunately brought me to an early stop of my Apple expedition.

I was just 18 years old when Apple introduced the Swift programming language at WWDC 2014. I followed the conference with great excitement, because the new features might give me new motivation for another expedition into the Apple cosmos. I was not disappointed. Swift looked great and I was directly motivated to dig into it. The timing also was perfect. I just bought a new MacBook, so I was not only motivated but also perfectly equipped. Shortly before my 19 birthday it was time and Apple published Siwft1. Learning Siwft was exciting and an extremely good addition to the programming languages and technologies I dealt with every day, primarily Java and web technologies. Later I studied the online lecture Stanford CS 193P by Paul Hegarty. Since then Swift is my goto language for private projects and excursions into new subject areas.

For example, when I made a private excursion into the field of computer graphics and the math behind it, Swift in combination with Metal was my valued companion. Hopefully there will be many more wonderful expeditions in the field of computer science, that I can be passioned about.
